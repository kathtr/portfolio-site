Refit

Become fit wherever.

About

Given the current situation of the pandemic, people are spending more time at home and are less active. This has not only physical but also severe psychological effects on us. ReFit offers a space where people can follow their personal fitness goals and become more active at home or wherever they wish.

ReFit is a native fitness app that helps users reach their personal fitness goals at home or wherever they are. Users can purchase customized workout plans based on the users’ profiles, track their daily and weekly progress, and watch workout videos to get a step closer to their goals.

Challenges

The main challenge in this project was the differentiation between Android and iOS design while keeping the overall structure consistent. I have never owned or worked with an Android phone before, so therefore dealing with its design structure and flow felt strange in the beginning.

To overcome the strange sensation, I studied the Material Design Guidelines, watched Android design tutorials and analyzed other designers’ works.

Competitor Anlysis

User Flow

Next on, I began to sketch out the user flow for the 3 functionalities that this case study is focusing on: Purchasing a subscription plan, track the workout progress and watch a workout video.

Sketches

Wireframes

Once I sketched out the first screens, I started to bring the corresponding mid-fidelity wireframes into life. To complete the user stories, I have created additional screens such as a “Loading” screen. As requested for this case study, I oriented myself towards the Human Interface and Material Design Guidelines.

From the competitor analysis, I understood how I want to present the screens and what I need to consider to improve the design flow. I wanted to shorten the onboarding screens and give users the ability to track their fitness progress in one single view.

Style Guide

Prototyping

After applying the style guide onto the wireframes, I then created an interactive prototype using Invision. I asked 7 testers to walk through the app and comment on the design and flow.

I was happy to hear that the test users found the app intuitive and did not find any big issues when navigating through it. A critique that was pointed out, was f.ex. that an element was not very visible, so I made the changes accordingly. You can find the prototypes here:

Final Design

Takeaways

As this case study was more focused on the UI design, the UX part came a bit short. I would have liked to study more about the users and their behavior. When I received the feedback for my prototypes, it included not only comments on the design but also wishes on functionalities or extra screens to complete the user flow, such as the ability to connect to GoogleFit, or to implement a feedback screen after the workout is finished. These are all great insights, that I would like to consider for a second iteration.

Overall, I think that I have managed well to keep the screens consistent throughout the two platforms while creating an intuitive design path and using the best practices of both Human Interface and Material Design guidelines.
